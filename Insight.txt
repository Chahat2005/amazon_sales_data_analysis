Amazon Product Data Analysis – Key Insights

1. Product Availability & Popular Categories
The analysis shows that product distribution is highly concentrated in a few categories. Certain categories dominate the dataset with significantly higher product listings, indicating Amazon’s strong focus on high-demand product segments.

2. Customer Engagement & Demand
Customer review analysis reveals that only a small number of categories receive the majority of reviews. This suggests that customer interest and demand are heavily focused on specific product segments.

3. Product Type Distribution
The dataset is unevenly distributed across product types, where a few product types make up the majority of listings while many others have limited representation. This indicates specialization in key product areas.

4. Pricing Strategy & Customer Affordability
Price distribution analysis shows that most products fall within the low to mid-price range. High-priced products represent only a small portion of the dataset, suggesting that Amazon primarily targets budget-conscious and mid-range customers.

5. Customer Satisfaction
The majority of products have ratings between 4.0 and 4.5, indicating generally positive customer feedback and overall satisfaction.

6. Discount Strategy Across Categories
Discount analysis reveals that some categories offer significantly higher average discounts than others. This suggests strong competition and price-sensitive markets within these segments.

7. Impact of Discount on Ratings
The relationship between discount percentage and product rating is weak. Higher discounts do not necessarily lead to better ratings, indicating that product quality and customer experience play a more important role than price reductions.
